Ferrenghi Island Chain Information

In order to ensure that the Kapulici Monkeys do not go extinct in the Ferrenghi Island Chain, your NGO aims to bring the Kapulici population in each main island back to Year 1 levels
by the end of Year 5
. Due to the islands in the Ferrenghi Island Chain being separated by the ocean,
inter-island movement is impossible
.
Relocation efforts by humans are not possible
to avoid breaking up Kapulici families.

The 3 main species on the island chain are: Kapulici Monkeys, Striped Anacondas, and Bushy Capybaras. The primary food source of Striped Anacondas are
Bushy Capybaras
; unfortunately, a
disease from Year 2 has decimated the Bushy Capybara population
,thereby
forcing Striped Anacondas to hunt more Kapulici Monkeys

By analyzing the population data for these 2 species, it was determined that, on average, a Striped Anaconda consumes
1 Kapulici Monkey a month in Rendang island
.

This varies island by island given varying Capybara populations, with the ratio Rendang Island : Feruni Island : Manggis Island : Nangi Island being
1: 1.2 : 2 : 1.5


Kapulici Monkeys are a relatively short-lived species with an average lifespan of just
10 years
.Historically, the Kapulici Monkey population on the Ferrenghi Chain have been
evenly split gender-wise
.
Five-sixths of females
will mate in a given year, giving birth to an
average of 1.5 babies annually
. Nonetheless, this is
constrained by availability of food

Kapulici Monkeys are picky eaters â€“ they only consume Ruby Bananas.
1 Ruby Banana tree can sustain a population of 8 Kapulici Monkeys
. Unfortunately, the number of Ruby Banana trees have also declined in recent years for unknown reasons.

Accordingly, your NGO has defined the following activities to be completed after the end of Year 4:
  • Relocate 8 Anacondas from each island to nearby islands outside the Ferrenghi Island Chain with more abundant food sources
  • Move 8 Ruby Banana trees in Rendang Island
  • Move 5 Ruby Banana trees in Feruni Island
  • Move 11 Ruby Banana trees in Manggis Island
